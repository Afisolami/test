Chief Funmilayo Ransome-Kuti

The Woman Who Fought and Saved a Billion Lives

Timeline of Chief Funmilayo Ransome-Kuti

  • 1900: Born in Abeokuta, Southern Nigeria

  • 1914: She attended Abeokuta Grammar School for her secondary education, in which she was among the the first six girls to be registered for study that year even though the school had initially been open for male students alone

  • 1919: She went abroad and attended a finishing school for girls in Cheshire, England, where she learned elocution,music,dressmaking,French and various domestic skills. She decided to use her shortened Yoruba name, Funmilayo, instead of her Christian name Frances

  • 1925: Funmilayo married Reverend Isreal Oludotun Ransome-Kuti family

  • 1932: Ransome-Kutihad helped establish the Abeokuta Ladies Club

  • 1935: The couple arranged to purchase a secondhand car and had it shipped to them from England. Ransome-Kutiwas the first woman in Abeokuta to drive a car

  • 1940: Ransome-Kuti established the Abeokuta Women's Union and advocated for women's right, demanding better representation of women in local governing bodies and an end to unfair taxes on market women

  • 1947: The National Council of Nigeria and the Cameroons party (NCNC) sent a delegation to London,England, to protest a proposed Nigerian constitution. Ransome-Kuti was the sole woman in the delegation

  • 1949: She proposed the creation of the Nigerian Women's Union (NWU) in order tto support women's rights and enfranchisement across the country

  • 1950: Ransome-Kutiwas appointed to the Western House of Chiefs and granted the chieftancy title of Oloye of the Yoruba people

  • 1955: Isreal Ransome-Kuti(husband) died from prostrate cancerafter an extended period of illness

  • 1978: Ransome-Kuti died following the attack that took place 1977 after visiting her son at his compound, in which close to 1000 armed soldiers surrounded and stormed the property. Fela(her son) was beaten and severly injured. Anikulapo-Kuti was thrown from a second floor window
